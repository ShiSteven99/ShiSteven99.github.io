Technical Applications

Cobot Application Domains
End-to-end integration across six industrial use cases

Every application started with the same question: where is the human constraint? In welding, a talent shortage stops small manufacturers from taking on larger contracts — deploying a cobot for repetitive passes frees skilled welders to focus on high-complexity joints, unlocking the capacity to grow. In machine tending and bin picking, the constraint is throughput consistency. The engineering work translated these business problems into reliable automated solutions.

Vision-Guided Bin Picking Palletizing Screwdriving Machine Tending Quality Inspection Cobot Welding
Communication Protocols & Integration
Hardware interfaces and industrial networking

Working across diverse hardware meant mastering the industrial communication landscape. Allen Bradley PLCs were integrated via Ethernet/IP; Siemens via Profinet. Vision systems typically communicated over TCP/IP, while simpler peripherals used digital I/O or Modbus. Knowing which protocol to reach for — and understanding the tradeoffs — was as important as the programming itself.
